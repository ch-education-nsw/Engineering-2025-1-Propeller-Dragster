One of the most prominent and promising upcoming methods of sustainable transport are the electrical vertical takeoff and landing (eVTOL) vehicles. eVTOL's aim is to completely remove the issue of traffic and fly at a conveniently fast speed, while remaining affordable enough for the average person. eVTOL's, while seeming futuristic and far-fetched, have had many advancements recently. On the 11th of March 2025, an eVTOL company named AVIC tested two new aircraft. Sources say it will have completed its maiden flight by the end of 2025. eVTOLs use high-density solid-state batteries to contain large amounts of energy in a small and light area. These new batteries charge fast, weigh less, last longer, and are much safer.

In the public transport sector, new technology for hydrogen-powered trains has been explored. New PEM (proton-exchange membrane) fuel cells have been developed for the trains. These fuel cells are able to contain much denser amounts of hydrogen in the same space, making the hydrogen route much more viable. One of the major drawbacks to hydrogen fuel is its tendency to explode and catch fire, causing much devastation.
